A framework for Agile Project Management

SCRUM is a lightweight framework that helps teams work together. It encourages teams to learn through experiences, self-organize while working on a problem, and reflect on their wins and losses to continuously improve.

About SCRUM

SCRUM is a framework used by teams to manage and complete complex projects, typically in software development. It divides the project into small, manageable iterations known as "sprints," which last typically between 1 and 4 weeks. SCRUM emphasizes collaboration, accountability, and iterative progress.

Benefits of SCRUM

  • Improved communication and collaboration within the team
  • Increased productivity and quality of deliverables
  • Flexibility and adaptability to change
  • Enhanced customer satisfaction through incremental progress

SCRUM Roles

SCRUM defines three main roles:

  • Product Owner: Represents the stakeholders and is responsible for ensuring that the team delivers value.
  • SCRUM Master: Facilitates the SCRUM process, ensuring that the team adheres to SCRUM practices and removes obstacles.
  • Development Team: A self-organizing group that works together to deliver the product incrementally.
